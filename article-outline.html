NON-LIFE INSURANCE MODELING

From Generalized Linear Models to Neural Networks

  • explains what non-life insurance is and what the goal is

  • explains that GLMs have been the main thing used by actuaries to discriminate policyholders for decades, and the potential issues that arise from using them. This leads to talking about using neural networks and their use in helping.

The Balance Property

  • explains what the EDF is for GLMs and why it is so useful, particularly that the balance property holds meaning the total premium charged over the entire portfolio equals the claim amount observed in the last accounting year

  • explains the importance of MLE in the actuarial field

  • explains that outliers in insurance are usually different from outliers in the general data science sense if that they are usually not data errors, but rather large financial claims that are important components in the pricing

Neural Networks and Representation Learning

  • explains the use of neural networks as an extension of GLMs

  • explains different processes for simplifying these networks

  • shows some critiques of neural networks, mainly on interpretability

Discrimination and Telematics Car Driving Data

  • explains that there are protected characteristics under the law that are not allowed to be used for insurance pricing (e.g. ethnicity)

  • explains that avoiding indirect discrimination is also important on top of avoiding direct discrimination

  • explains that this problem could be circumvented with personal and behavior data, but that there are major privacy issues with this
